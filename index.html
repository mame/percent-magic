percent-magic: rational approximation table of percentages

What is this?

What impression do you get when you hear that “71.4% of those surveyed were in favor?” If you imagine that 1,000 people were surveyed and 714 were in favor, it might sound like a solid majority.

However, the 71.4% figure could also be expressed as 5 / 7. In other words, the survey might have involved just 7 people, with only 5 in favor. Does that feel misleading?

This teaches us an important lesson: we should be cautious of surveys that do not disclose their sample sizes.

This page provides a table of the simplest rational number approximation to various percentages.
